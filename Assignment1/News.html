  1. Australia skipper Pat Cummins confirmed that the team would make changes for the fourth Ashes Test in Manchester. Australia are going in with an all-out pace attack at Old Trafford for the crucial fourth Test of the Ashes series.   2. Saud Shakeel made an unbeaten double century to put Pakistan in the driving seat in the Galle Test at stumps on day three. Shakeel completed a double hundred while batting alongside No.11 Abrar Ahmed and remained unbeaten on 208 when the last wicket fell. Pakistan took a decisive 149-run lead that could prove to be crucial as the Test goes on. The Pakistan middle-order batter resumed on 69* and continued the visitors' recovery from 101/5. The pair were separated with the score on 278 when Agha Salman fell for 83, but Shakeel carried on for Pakistan.
  3. After Sri Lanka and Netherlands booked their place in the ICC Men's Cricket World Cup 2023 through the Qualifier in Zimbabwe, the updated schedule for the marquee event in India is now out. Sri Lanka and Netherlands were the two teams to make it through to the ICC Men's Cricket World Cup 2023 in India. Sri Lanka beat Netherlands in the Qualifier final in Harare and will open their World Cup campaign against South Africa at the Arun Jaitley Stadium in New Delhi on 7 October. They finish off their Group Stage campaign against New Zealand in Bengaluru on 9 November.
